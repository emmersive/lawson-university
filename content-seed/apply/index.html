How to Apply

Tabs
Domestic Students

Applying as a Domestic Student

Domestic students (Australian citizens, permanent residents, and New Zealand citizens) apply through the Victorian Tertiary Admissions Centre (VTAC) for undergraduate programs, or directly through Lawson for postgraduate programs.

Undergraduate via VTAC

  1. Create a VTAC account at vtac.edu.au
  2. Add Lawson University courses to your preferences
  3. Submit your application by the VTAC deadline
  4. Accept your offer through VTAC
  5. Enrol through the Lawson Student Portal

Postgraduate Direct Application

  1. Check your eligibility and course requirements
  2. Gather required documents (transcripts, CV, references)
  3. Submit your application via the Lawson Application Portal
  4. Receive an outcome within 10 business days
Start your application
International Students

Applying as an International Student

International students apply directly to Lawson University through our online application portal. Our International Admissions team will guide you through every step.

  1. Check entry requirements for your chosen program
  2. Confirm your English language proficiency scores
  3. Prepare supporting documents (academic transcripts, passport, language scores)
  4. Submit your application through the Lawson Application Portal
  5. Receive your Letter of Offer (typically within 2–4 weeks)
  6. Accept your offer and pay your deposit
  7. Receive your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)
  8. Apply for your Student Visa (subclass 500)
Apply as international student
Postgraduate Research

Applying for a Research Degree

PhD and Master of Research applications are made directly to Lawson's Graduate Research Office. You'll need to identify a potential supervisor before applying.

  1. Explore research areas and identify a potential supervisor
  2. Contact the supervisor to discuss your research proposal
  3. Prepare your research proposal (2,000–5,000 words)
  4. Submit your application with all required documents
  5. Attend an interview if shortlisted
  6. Receive an outcome within 4–6 weeks
