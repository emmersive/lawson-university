Our Story

Founded in 1965 by a group of visionary academics and civic leaders, Lawson University was built on the belief that higher education should be transformative — for individuals, communities, and the world.

Named after pioneering Australian author Henry Lawson, our University has grown from a single campus in Clayton to a multi-campus institution serving over 52,000 students from 140 countries.

Today, Lawson is ranked among the top 80 universities globally, with a reputation for groundbreaking research, exceptional teaching, and a deep commitment to sustainability and social justice.

Our Leadership

Cards

Professor Sarah Chen

Vice-Chancellor & President
Professor Chen joined Lawson in 2021 from the University of Cambridge, where she led the Faculty of Engineering. A globally recognised leader in sustainable technology policy, she holds fellowships with the Australian Academy of Science and the Royal Society.

Professor James Okafor

Deputy Vice-Chancellor, Research
Professor Okafor oversees Lawson's $180M+ research portfolio. A neuroscientist by training, he has published over 200 peer-reviewed papers and leads the University's partnerships with the Australian Research Council.

Dr Michelle Park

Deputy Vice-Chancellor, Education
Dr Park drives Lawson's innovative curriculum design and digital learning strategy. Her work on personalised learning pathways has attracted international recognition and been adopted by universities in 12 countries.

Sustainability Commitment

Columns

Carbon Neutral by 2030

Lawson is committed to achieving carbon neutrality across all campuses by 2030. Our Sustainability Strategy 2025–2030 outlines our roadmap to net zero, including 100% renewable electricity, EV fleet transition, and sustainable building certification for all new construction.

View our sustainability strategy

Social Impact

Ranked #1 in Australia for social impact by the Times Higher Education Impact Rankings, Lawson actively works to close the gap in higher education participation, support First Nations students, and engage with our local communities through outreach and partnership programs.
